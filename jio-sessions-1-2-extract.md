## Session 2: Customer Experience Vision (Matt Rosenblatt, Alex Kaplan, Mark)

### AI-Native Telco Principles

**Foundational questions that framed the vision:**
- What if the best CX metric is how many problems AI **prevents**, not resolves?
- What if AI turns pricing into **personalized negotiation on my behalf**?
- What if AI collapses the boundary between network, service, and experience?
- What if differentiation is **radical predictability**, not better service?
- What if AI replaces journeys and personas with **experience orchestration**?

**Characteristics of an AI-native telco:**
1. **Experience IS the product** — not a manifestation of the product
2. **Network as programmable platform** — predicted by default
3. **Real-time continuous decisions** — moment-led, not function-led
4. **Customer of one** — not personas, but contextual individual
5. **Autonomy with accountability** — transparent AI roadmap, guardrails
6. **Self-healing, self-configuring, self-protecting**

### The Three Horizons

| Horizon | What | Customer interaction | Status |
|---------|------|---------------------|--------|
| **H1: Enhance** | AI layered into existing processes | Structured conversations, existing touchpoints (contact center, app) | Where most global telcos are today |
| **H2: Intent-Driven** | "I state what I want" in natural language | Customer expresses intent freely, system acts | Tech is ready, adoption shifting |
| **H3: Agent-to-Agent** | My agent identifies needs and acts for me | Agent operates on my behalf, negotiates, transacts | Tech emerging, trust is the bottleneck |

**Critical insight on the shift:**
> "These aren't sequential. You need action and investment in all three right now."
> "The ChatGPT education of communication — customers now use more words, are more expansive in describing what they want. The nature of the conversation has shifted."

## Demo Highlights & Reactions

### 1. Browser Agent Negotiating Broadband (Mark)
**What:** Used a browser agent (Anthropic/Complexity) to find the best broadband deal and negotiate with a human agent at a UK telco.

**Key moments:**
- Agent took over screen, did market research, found competitive deals, defined its own negotiation strategy
- When it reached the chatbot, it demanded escalation to a human — "comical because an AI agent insisted on talking to a human"
- Got a deal *below the threshold* that human agents are authorized to offer
- Human agents checked with supervisors — couldn't tell it was an AI
- **Total time: ~2 minutes from prompt to engagement**

**Jio reaction:** Deep interest. This is happening NOW, not future.

**Mark's data point:** European telco analysis shows complaints have become dramatically more sophisticated in last 2 years because consumers use AI to craft them.

> *"We have the best informed consumers we've ever had. They've got tooling that is available to them which is as good [as what companies have]. This agency future sometimes feels like it's a long way away. Reality is that it's here."*

### 2. Guardian Mesh — Voice Parental Controls (Mark, live demo)
**What:** Parent speaks a voice note to lock down children's devices during study time.

**Key moments:**
- Single voice prompt → system identifies TWO requests (two children, not just one)
- Translates into API requests → creates network rules → executes
- Rules apply at network level, not app level
- Voice as dominant future interaction form — multi-lingual capability critical for India market

**Jio reaction (strong):**
- "We should be bold... aspire to Horizon 3... not sequential phases"
- "Can we do a 20, 30, 40 day sprint?"
- Jio claimed they already provide similar services (Ask My WiFi, setup, content controls) — "the enablers are on the enemy side [our side]"

**Key moments:**
